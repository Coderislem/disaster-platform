Essential Wildfire Information

What is a Wildfire?

A wildfire is an uncontrolled fire that burns in wildland vegetation, often in rural areas.

Warning Signs

Smoke, unusual heat, red skies, and emergency alerts are key indicators of approaching wildfires.

Danger Levels

Wildfires can spread at speeds of up to 14 mph and reach temperatures of over 2,000Â°F.

Property Impact

Wildfires can destroy homes and create dangerous air quality conditions for miles around.

Before, During & After a Wildfire

Before a Wildfire

  • Create defensible space around your home
  • Prepare an emergency kit
  • Develop an evacuation plan
  • Install fire-resistant materials
  • Clear vegetation near buildings

During a Wildfire

  • Follow evacuation orders immediately
  • Stay tuned to emergency broadcasts
  • Keep windows and doors closed
  • Use N95 masks for smoke
  • Stay hydrated

After a Wildfire

  • Wait for official clearance to return
  • Check for hot spots
  • Document damage
  • Watch for flash floods
  • Monitor air quality
