Earthquake Safety Guide

Essential Earthquake Information

What is an Earthquake?

An earthquake is a sudden release of energy in Earth's crust that creates seismic waves.

Warning Signs

Foreshocks, unusual animal behavior, and ground deformation can precede major earthquakes.

Magnitude Scale

The Richter scale measures earthquake intensity, with each number representing a 10-fold increase.

Structural Impact

Earthquakes can damage buildings, trigger landslides, and cause devastating tsunamis.

Before, During & After an Earthquake

Before an Earthquake

  • Secure heavy furniture
  • Create emergency kit
  • Identify safe spots
  • Practice Drop, Cover, Hold
  • Know utility shutoffs

During an Earthquake

  • Drop, Cover, and Hold On
  • Stay away from windows
  • If in bed, stay there
  • Avoid doorways
  • Wait for shaking to stop

After an Earthquake

  • Check for injuries
  • Watch for aftershocks
  • Monitor news updates
  • Inspect for damage
  • Avoid damaged areas
