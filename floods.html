Essential Flood Information

What is a Flood?

A flood occurs when water overflows onto normally dry land. It's one of the most common natural disasters globally.

Warning Signs

Heavy rainfall, rising water levels, and weather alerts are key indicators of potential flooding.

Danger Levels

Just 6 inches of moving water can knock you down, and 2 feet can sweep away most vehicles.

Property Impact

Floods can cause severe structural damage and create dangerous electrical hazards in homes.

Before, During & After a Flood

Before a Flood

  • Create an emergency kit
  • Develop an evacuation plan
  • Install flood barriers
  • Keep important documents safe
  • Monitor weather updates

During a Flood

  • Move to higher ground
  • Avoid walking in water
  • Don't drive in flooded areas
  • Stay informed via radio
  • Follow evacuation orders

After a Flood

  • Wait for official clearance
  • Document damage
  • Clean and disinfect
  • Check for structural damage
  • Contact insurance company
